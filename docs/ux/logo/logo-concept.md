# StudyPuck Logo Concept

## Direction: The Pansy (Love-in-Idleness)

The StudyPuck logo is a five-petaled pansy — the same flower Puck fetches in *A Midsummer Night's Dream*. This makes the mark deeply thematic: Puck squeezes the juice of this flower onto sleeping eyes, causing them to fall in love with whatever they first see upon waking. It is an apt metaphor for a vocabulary learning app that makes you fall in love with words.

> *"The juice of it on sleeping eyelids laid / Will make or man or woman madly dote / Upon the next live creature that it sees."*  
> — Oberon, Act II Scene i

Shakespeare also describes it as *"milk-white, now purple with love's wound"* — which directly inspired the two-tone petal palette.

## Mark

**File:** `docs/ux/logo/pansy-mark.svg`  
**Source:** Created in [Recraft.ai](https://recraft.ai) — organic vector paths, 1024×1024 coordinate space.  
**Backup (original export):** `docs/ux/logo/pansy-mark-recraft-original.svg`

The mark uses five petals in the brand's two accent colors:
- **3 petals** — Warm Burgundy `#8b1a1a` (the "wound")
- **2 petals** — Deep Violet `#5c2d91` (the "purple" of the flower)
- **Gold center** — `#e8b800` (stamens)

## Favicon

**File:** `apps/web/static/favicon.svg`  
**Variant:** B1 — tighter viewBox crop (`250 150 530 680`) applied to the master paths, with a white keyline around the petal shapes for 16-32px readability on dark browser chrome.

At 16–32px the tighter crop zooms in on the petal cluster, making the flower readable at small sizes. The white keyline keeps the petal cracks and outer silhouette legible against dark tabs while preserving the gold center and original Recraft paths.

## Usage Notes

- The mark should always appear on a light (`#faf6f2`) or dark (`#0d1117`) background — never on busy or colored backgrounds.
- Minimum rendered size: **16px** (favicon). Below this the mark becomes illegible.
- The mark is designed as a standalone icon; a full wordmark lockup (mark + "StudyPuck" in Playfair Display) is shown in the session design reference.
- All SVG paths are pure geometry — no embedded fonts. Safe to use as standalone SVG anywhere.
